The Problem Nobody Talks About

Your content is not bad.
It just sounds like nobody's.

I look at small business content every single day. And I keep noticing the same thing. The business is real. The work is good. The owner is working hard. But the content could belong to any of their competitors. That is not a posting problem. That is a voice problem.

01

You are trying to speak to everyone.

So nobody feels like you are speaking to them. When a post works for a 22-year-old student and a 50-year-old CEO equally, it resonates with neither.

02

Your brand sounds different depending on the day.

Professional Monday. Casual Wednesday. Motivational Friday. Readers cannot build a picture of who you are. So they scroll past without registering you exist.

03

You describe what you do instead of what changes.

"High quality services at competitive prices." Every business says this. None of them say what actually shifts for the customer who chooses them.

04

You post. You get silence. You do not know why.

It is not the algorithm. It is not the time you posted. It is that nobody reading feels like this was written for them specifically. That is fixable. Starting now.

"The fix is not posting more. It is knowing exactly who you are talking to and what your business genuinely sounds like."

Step One

Get specific about
one real person.

Not a target audience. Not a demographic. One actual human being who has a problem your business solves. When a business gets genuinely clear on this, everything changes. The captions sharpen. The DMs get replies. Here is how to find that person.

What keeps them up at night?

Not the surface problem. The one underneath. The thing they have not told anyone but scroll past thinking about. If you can name it before they do, they will read every word you write.

"My customer lies awake worrying about ________________________________"

What do they feel when they see a competitor doing well?

This one is uncomfortable but important. Frustration? The fear of falling behind? That emotion is your hook. Name it in your content and watch what happens to your engagement.

"When they see a competitor winning, they feel _____________________________"

What does their life look like after working with you?

Not your service. The transformation. What stops being a problem? What becomes possible? What do they finally stop worrying about? This is your real offer. Everything you write points here.

"After working with me, my customer finally ________________________________"
✗ Without this clarity

"We help businesses of all sizes reach their full potential with tailored solutions." Nobody. This is for nobody.

✓ With this clarity

"You have been running this for two years. The work is good. But your competitors keep getting the clients. That ends now."

Step Two

Figure out what your
business actually sounds like.

Most businesses do not have a posting problem. They have a clarity problem. They have never sat down and asked: if my business were a person walking into a room, how would they talk? Here is how you answer that honestly.

Three words. How do you want your business to feel?

Not your values. The actual feeling someone gets in the first 10 seconds of reading your content. Think of a brand you love — they make you feel something specific instantly. What do you want yours to be? Write three honest words.

My business should feel: _______________ / _______________ / _______________

What will you never sound like — and why?

Your voice is shaped as much by what you reject as what you claim. Most businesses accidentally copy brands they admire and end up sounding like everyone else. Name the tone you will never use — and understand exactly why your clients deserve better than that.

I will never sound like _______________ because my clients deserve ___________

If your best client described you to a friend, what would they say?

Not what you want them to say. What they actually say. The real words a real person uses when they trust someone. Write that sentence. Then build every single post toward making it true.

"You should work with her, she is the one who ______________________________"
✗ Without a clear voice

"At our organization we are committed to excellence and delivering outstanding results for all stakeholders." Nobody believes this. Nobody remembers it.

✓ With a clear voice

"I kept watching good businesses get ignored online while average ones with louder voices won. So I help the good ones get heard." You remember this one.

Step Three — Do This Today

Now write one post
that actually means something.

You have done the thinking. You know who you are writing for and what your business sounds like. Now put it together. This is the exact structure I use when writing for any business — because it starts with the person, not the product.

Step 1

Line 1 — Name their problem.

Use what you wrote in Q1 on page 3. The thing they wake up thinking about. Do not soften it. Do not add context first. Just say it. That first line is the only thing standing between them scrolling past and stopping to read.

Step 2

Lines 2 to 5 — Show you understand why it happens.

Not the solution yet. First you prove you get it. Write in your voice from page 4. Warm and direct if that is what you chose. Sharp and honest if that is yours. This is where they go from "interesting" to "this person gets my situation."

Step 3

Last line — Name the transformation.

Use Q3 from page 3. What their life looks like after working with you. Not a call to action. Not "DM me." The actual result. One sentence. Specific enough to feel real. That line is what makes them reach out.
